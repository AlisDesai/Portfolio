# Portfolio Overview

A quick, non-technical guide to what this website is and what it contains — written for anyone (clients, partners, non-developers) who wants to understand the site without digging into code.

> **Note:** The site's current content (footer signature, contact form, email, giant background text) is branded entirely around **Harsh** as a solo freelance developer. There is no mention of **Alis** anywhere in the visible content yet. If this is meant to be a joint "Alis & Harsh" partner portfolio, the branding, footer, contact details, and copy will need to be updated to reflect both names.

## What this site is

A premium, portfolio/marketing website for a freelance **backend & mobile app developer**, designed to attract potential clients, showcase past work, and make it easy for visitors to start a project inquiry. The look and feel is dark, moody, and editorial on the home page, switching to a bright, clean, airy style on the inner pages — meant to feel high-end and trustworthy rather than like a typical template.

Based in **Ahmedabad/Vadodara, India**, but positioned as **"Available Worldwide."**

## Pages

- **Home** — The main landing page. Big animated intro headline, a rotating showcase of flagship projects, a "serving businesses worldwide" stats section (with a world map and animated counters), an overview of services, a scrolling marquee of search-style phrases (like "need a backend developer" — subtle SEO/credibility messaging), client testimonials, and a closing call-to-action to get in touch.
- **Work** — A "Selected Works" page listing project case studies (title, category, short description, tech used) with a cursor-following image preview as you browse the list.
- **Services** — A dedicated breakdown of what's offered, plus a "why work with me" trust section and a simplified contact prompt.
- **Contact** — An inquiry page built around a playful, conversational fill-in-the-blank form (e.g. *"Hi Harsh, my name is ___ and I'm looking for help with ___..."*), along with project-interest tags, budget/timeline pickers, a file attachment box, and a send button.
